🏗️ BMRDA Layout Property Guide

Complete End-to-End Process & Document Checklist

What is BMRDA?

Bangalore Metropolitan Region Development Authority

Established: 2007 (under BMRDA Act 1985)

Coverage: 8,005 sq. km across Bangalore Urban, Bangalore Rural, and Ramanagara districts

Key Difference from BDA: BMRDA doesn't acquire land but approves projects that meet strict infrastructure and development guidelines.

✅ What BMRDA Approval Means

  • Layout verified for proper road width, drainage, parks
  • Adherence to land use zoning regulations
  • Legal and safe for investment
  • Eligible for property registration and utility connections
  • Compliance with minimum standards (road width, park allocation, OSR, drainage)

⚠️ Important Notes

  • BMRDA doesn't directly approve layouts - Local Planning Authorities (LPAs) do
  • Always verify approval directly with the issuing LPA
  • Non-approved or revenue layouts lack essential infrastructure and legal protection
  • Check if your property falls under BMRDA approved layout list before purchasing

BMRDA vs BDA vs BBMP

Authority Jurisdiction Cost
BDA Bangalore Urban (city limits) Highest
BMRDA Outside BDA limits (metropolitan region) Moderate
BBMP Greater Bengaluru civic body Varies

⚙️ End-to-End Process

1
Property Jurisdiction Verification

Action: Confirm whether your property falls under BMRDA jurisdiction (outside BDA limits)

Check: Location should be in Bangalore Urban/Rural/Ramanagara districts but outside BBMP/BDA limits

Identify: Which Local Planning Authority (LPA) governs your area:

  • Anekal Planning Authority
  • Hoskote Planning Authority
  • Nelamangala Planning Authority
  • Bangalore Bidadi Smart City Planning Authority (BIAAPA)
  • Magadi Planning Authority
  • Kanakapura Planning Authority

2
Land Preparation & Conversion (if needed)

For Agricultural Land:

  • Obtain Conversion Order to convert agricultural land to non-agricultural
  • If zoning differs from intended use, apply for Change of Land Use (CLU)
  • CLU approval comes from Chief Minister's Office via BDA/BMRDA/LPA
  • Timeline: 6-9 months for CLU
3
Layout Plan Preparation

Engage: Licensed architect or planner familiar with BMRDA regulations

Design must include:

  • Minimum road width compliance
  • Park allocation as per norms
  • Open Space Reservation (OSR)
  • Proper drainage provisions
  • Plot divisions and dimensions
  • Setback requirements
  • FAR (Floor Area Ratio) compliance

Note: Layout sanction required if land area exceeds 2.5 acres

4
Document Collection & Verification

Gather all required documents (see Documents tab)

Key verifications:

  • Encumbrance Certificate check (13-30 years)
  • Title deed chain verification
  • Survey number matching
  • Boundary verification
  • Khata certificate validation
5
Application Submission

Fill BMRDA/LPA application form manually

Submit package includes:

  • Completed application form with signatures
  • All ownership and title documents
  • Approved layout plan blueprints
  • Sanction drawings per zoning regulations
  • Affidavits as required
  • ID proofs and photographs

Submit to: Relevant LPA office (not BMRDA directly)

6
Document & Drawing Verification

Authority Review: LPA officials verify all documents and drawings

Checks include:

  • Compliance with zoning regulations
  • Road width, drainage, park allocations
  • Survey number accuracy
  • Legal ownership validation
  • Building bye-laws compliance

Timeline: 2-4 weeks typically

7
Site Inspection

Physical Verification: Engineer or case worker visits the site

Inspection covers:

  • Physical boundaries match approved plan
  • Road access verification
  • Infrastructure availability check
  • Drainage and water provisions
  • No encroachments or disputes
8
Fee Payment

Receive: Demand notes/challans for various fees

Fee components include:

  • Application fees
  • Scrutiny charges
  • Development charges
  • Betterment charges
  • 1% labor cess

Note: Fees vary by plot size and building type (see Fees tab)

Payment: Pay at designated bank/online and collect receipt

9
Approval Issuance

Receive: Building plan approval and sanction letter from LPA

Documents issued:

  • Layout Approval Certificate
  • Sanctioned plan copy
  • Approval number for reference

Total Timeline: 30-45 days from complete application submission

10
Construction & Completion (if building)

During Construction:

  • Follow approved plans strictly
  • Major deviations require re-approval
  • Maintain construction permits on-site

After Completion:

  • Apply for Occupancy Certificate (OC)
  • Final inspection by authorities
  • OC mandatory for buildings > G+3 floors
  • For plotted developments: Obtain Release Order (issued in 2 tranches at 40% and 60% completion)

✅ Post-Approval Benefits

  • Legal construction rights
  • Property registration enabled
  • Eligible for bank loans
  • Utility connections (water, electricity, sewage)
  • Higher resale value
  • Protection from demolition notices

🏛️ BMRDA & Local Planning Authorities

Understanding the Structure

BMRDA itself doesn't directly approve individual layouts. Approvals are issued by Local Planning Authorities (LPAs) under BMRDA's jurisdiction. There are 13 LPAs within the Bangalore Metropolitan Region.

Local Planning Authorities Under BMRDA

1. Anekal Planning Authority

Coverage: Anekal Taluk area

Key Areas: Electronic City surroundings, Chandapura, Attibele region

Contact: Visit BMRDA office or respective LPA office for contact details

2. Hoskote Planning Authority

Coverage: Hoskote Taluk area

Key Areas: Hoskote, Whitefield outskirts, industrial belt

3. Nelamangala Planning Authority

Coverage: Nelamangala Taluk area

Key Areas: NH-4 corridor, Tumkur Road surroundings

4. BIAAPA (Bangalore International Airport Area Planning Authority)

Coverage: Airport region and surroundings

Key Areas: Devanahalli, airport vicinity developments

Special Note: Special planning authority for strategic airport area development

5. Magadi Planning Authority

Coverage: Magadi Taluk area

Key Areas: Magadi Road corridor

6. Kanakapura Planning Authority

Coverage: Kanakapura Taluk area

Key Areas: Kanakapura Road, Nice Road corridor

7. Bangalore Bidadi Smart City Planning Authority (BMICAPA)

Coverage: Bidadi and surrounding smart city development area

Key Areas: Bidadi industrial area, Ramanagara district portions

⚠️ How to Identify Your LPA

  1. Check your property's exact location and taluk
  2. Visit BMRDA website or office with property survey number
  3. Ask your property developer/seller which LPA governs the area
  4. Cross-verify by visiting the LPA office or calling them

Other Related Authorities You May Encounter

Note: Always verify contact information from official government sources as these may change.

✅ How to Verify BMRDA Approval

🚨 Critical Importance

Always verify BMRDA/LPA approval BEFORE purchasing any property. Non-approved or fake layouts are common and can lead to:

  • Loss of investment
  • Legal disputes and court cases
  • Demolition notices
  • No utility connections
  • Inability to get bank loans
  • No legal construction rights

Step-by-Step Verification Process

1
Request Approval Certificate

Ask seller/developer for:

  • Official Layout Approval Certificate
  • Approval number clearly mentioned
  • Name of issuing LPA (Anekal, Hoskote, etc.)
  • Approval date
  • Copy of approved layout plan

Red Flag: If seller cannot provide these immediately, proceed with extreme caution

2
Visit LPA Office

Physical verification is essential:

  • Visit the relevant LPA office with certificate copy
  • Speak with Commissioner or designated official
  • Cross-verify approval number in their records
  • Check if layout appears in approved list
  • Confirm survey numbers match

Don't rely solely on online checks - physical verification is crucial

3
Check Online Records

Visit BMRDA/LPA websites:

  • Go to bmrda.karnataka.gov.in
  • Check for published lists of approved layouts
  • Verify layout is NOT in unauthorized/illegal list
  • Some LPAs have online verification portals

Note: Not all LPAs have complete online databases - office visit remains necessary

4
Document Cross-Verification

Check consistency across all documents:

  • Survey numbers match in EC, sale deed, layout plan
  • Plot numbers/CA site numbers are consistent
  • Boundaries and dimensions match site plan
  • Road widths match approved layout
  • Park/amenity allocations are as per approval

Any mismatch = Major Red Flag

5
Physical Site Inspection

Visit the actual site with a licensed surveyor:

  • Verify physical boundaries match plan
  • Check if roads are laid as per approved width
  • Confirm drainage systems exist
  • Parks/open spaces allocated as approved
  • No encroachments on your plot
  • Infrastructure promised actually exists

Many fraudulent layouts show perfect plans but have no ground reality

6
Legal Due Diligence

Engage a property lawyer to verify:

  • Encumbrance Certificate (EC) for 13-30 years
  • Title deed chain of ownership
  • No pending litigation or court cases
  • No mortgages or third-party claims
  • Conversion orders (if agricultural land)
  • All NOCs obtained properly
7
Bank/Financial Institution Check

If taking a loan:

  • Banks conduct independent legal verification
  • Will not approve loans for non-approved layouts
  • Bank rejection is a strong warning signal
  • Use bank's technical team's assessment as additional verification

🚩 Red Flags to Watch For

  • Seller cannot produce original approval certificate
  • Approval certificate looks fake or poorly photocopied
  • LPA office has no record of approval number
  • Layout appears in unauthorized/illegal list
  • Survey numbers don't match across documents
  • Physical site doesn't match approved plan
  • Roads narrower than approved width
  • Missing parks or open spaces shown in plan
  • Seller pressures you to skip verification
  • Price is suspiciously low compared to genuine approved layouts
  • Encumbrance Certificate shows disputes or mortgages
  • Agricultural land without proper conversion order

✅ Documents to Verify

  1. Sale Deed / Mother Deed - Registered and genuine
  2. Title Deed - Clear ownership chain
  3. Layout Approval Certificate - From correct LPA with valid number
  4. Approved Layout Plan - Matches physical site
  5. Encumbrance Certificate - 13-30 years, clean
  6. Khata Certificate - Up-to-date and valid
  7. Conversion Order - If previously agricultural
  8. Tax Receipts - Current and paid

✅ Best Practices

  • Hire a reputable property lawyer for due diligence

  • Check with neighbors about layout's history
  • If buying from developer, check their track record
  • Avoid properties with ongoing litigation
  • Get everything in writing
  • Don't rush - proper verification takes time
  • When in doubt, walk away
